Webtrends Infinity Reporting

Problem

In order to assess their digital properties, users need up-to-the-minute analytics data. Moreover they need to be able to explorer that data in many different ways: adding dimensions, comparing date ranges, comparing audience segments, and more.

Solution

  1. We began by evaluating user needs. That initial discovery period included user interviews, competitive analysis, and research of existing feedback from emails, forums, and direct contact. We then formed four user personas and their respective stories. The result was an extensive list of requirements.
  2. With stories, personas, and requirements in hand we began sketching user flows and information architecture.
  3. With positive feedback regarding flows we next moved to interface design, first on paper and then digitally.
  4. Using digital wireframes many interactions were tested with users, either in person or remotely. The tests were recorded, analyzed, iterated upon, and repeated.
  5. Interactions and general layouts were elaborated with interface developers.
  6. Pixel-specific designs were created and specifications were written.
  7. Development began. Interface developers, database developers, and I worked closely to build each feature and components in semi-agile 14-day iterations. Each iteration was concluded with a proof.
  8. A beta version of Infinity Reporting was released to specific customers. Feedback was collected, assessed, and influenced the next stages of development.

My Role

As Lead Designer on this project I was involved in every aspect of the process and was responsible for most of the details listed above. I conducted research, administered usability tests, designed wireframes, executed final designs, developed CSS, and evaluated customer feedback.
